SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT
This SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT (this "Agreement") is entered into as of
January 5, 2026 (the "Effective Date"), by and between (a) LandRight AI, Inc., a Delaware
corporation ("Client"); and (b) NexGen Software Solutions LLC, a Texas limited liability company
("Developer"). Client and Developer may be referred to herein individually as a "Party" and
collectively as the "Parties."
I. Purpose and Exhibits
1.1 Purpose. Client desires to engage Developer to design, develop, and deliver a minimum viable
product version of Client's "Eminent Domain AI" software platform, a legal-first project
management solution for private-sector eminent domain and land acquisition (the "MVP"), and
Developer desires to perform such services, in accordance with the terms of this Agreement.
1.2 Exhibits. The following exhibits are incorporated herein by this reference:
Exhibit A – Product Requirements Document
Exhibit B – Milestones, Schedule and Fees
Exhibit C – Change Request Procedure
In the event of a conflict between the terms of an Exhibit and the main body of this Agreement,
the main body shall control, unless expressly stated otherwise in such Exhibit.
II. Definitions
2.1 "Deliverables" means all software, documentation, configurations, scripts, specifications,
interfaces, designs, and other work product to be created or delivered by Developer in connection
with this Agreement, including the MVP as more fully described in Exhibit A and Exhibit B.
2.2 "Milestones" means the project phases set forth in Exhibit B, each with associated
Deliverables, Target Completion Dates, and Milestone Fees.
2.3 "Project Start Date" means the date mutually agreed by the Parties in writing on which
Developer commences work under this Agreement, which shall be no later than five (5) days
following the Effective Date, unless otherwise agreed.
2.4 "Acceptance Criteria" means the criteria for determining whether a Deliverable conforms to
the applicable requirements, as described in Exhibit A and Exhibit B.
2.5 "MVP Term" means the period commencing on the Project Start Date and ending upon the
earlier of (a) completion and acceptance of all Milestones, or (b) five (5) months from the Project
Start Date, subject to any mutually agreed extensions.
III. Services and Scope
3.1 Services. Developer shall perform software design, development, configuration, integration,
testing, and related services (the "Services") necessary to deliver the MVP in accordance with this
Agreement, Exhibit A (Product Requirements Document) and Exhibit B (Milestones, Schedule
and Fees).
3.2 MVP Scope. Without limiting Exhibit A, the MVP shall, at a minimum, include:
(a) Core project and parcel management (projects, parcels with PostGIS geometry);
(b) Legal-first notices & service engine (notices, service attempts, basic per-jurisdiction
rule validation);
(c) Right-of-Entry (ROE) management (templates, effective/expiry dates, access windows,
field check-in/out);
(d) Litigation calendar (quick-take vs standard flag with manually managed events and
deadlines);
(e) Title & curative tracking (title document metadata, curative items and status);
(f) Payment ledger (status-only, no valuation or dollar amounts);
(g) Parcel communications and basic audit events; and
(h) GIS alignment and parcel segmentation (alignment import, segment generation, per-
segment ED status).
3.3 Exclusions. The following are expressly out of scope for the MVP unless added via a Change
Request:
(a) Any valuation, appraisal, or compensation computations;
(b) URA/HUD relocation benefit calculators;
(c) Full environmental and NEPA compliance modules; and
(d) Advanced deadline engines or automated rule libraries beyond what is specified in
Exhibit A.
3.4 Standard of Performance. Developer shall perform the Services in a professional and
workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards for enterprise-grade SaaS development.
IV. Project Schedule; Milestones and Acceptance
4.1 Project Duration. The Parties agree that the MVP is intended to be completed within five (5)
months from the Project Start Date, subject to Client's timely cooperation and any approved
Change Requests.
4.2 Milestones. The Parties agree to the Milestones described in Exhibit B, which include, at a
minimum:
● Milestone 1 – Technical Design & Product Refinement;
● Milestone 2 – Backend MVP API;
● Milestone 3 – Frontend Internal Web App;
● Milestone 4 – Landowner Portal & Hardening; and
● Milestone 5 – UAT Completion & Production-Ready Package.
4.3 Acceptance Procedure.
(a) For each Milestone, Developer shall notify the Client in writing (email sufficient) that
the Milestone Deliverables are ready for review.
(b) Client shall have seven (7) business days from receipt of such notice (the "Acceptance
Period") to test the Deliverables against the Acceptance Criteria.
(c) If the Deliverables materially conform, Client shall issue a written acceptance or shall
be deemed to have accepted the Deliverables at the end of the Acceptance Period if no
rejection notice is sent.
(d) If the Deliverables do not materially conform, Client shall provide a written rejection
specifying the deficiencies with reasonable detail. Developer shall use commercially
reasonable efforts to correct such deficiencies within a mutually agreed cure period, after
which the Acceptance Procedure shall be repeated for the relevant Deliverables.
(e) Minor non-conformities that do not materially impair core functionality shall not be
grounds for withholding Milestone acceptance, and such items shall be prioritized as
defects to be resolved in subsequent sprints or as part of hardening/UAT.
V. Fees and Payment
5.1 Project Fee. In consideration for the Services and Deliverables, Client shall pay Developer a
fixed fee of One Hundred Seventy-Five Thousand Dollars (US $175,000.00) (the "Project Fee"),
payable as set forth in this Section 5.
5.2 Payment Schedule. The Project Fee shall be allocated and payable as follows:
(a) Mobilization Payment. Twenty percent (20%) of the Project Fee ($35,000) is due
within three (3) business days following the Effective Date and in any event prior to
Developer assigning a project team and commencing work under this Agreement.
(b) Milestone 1 – Technical Design & Product Refinement. Upon Client's written
acceptance of Milestone 1 Deliverables in accordance with Section 4.3, twenty percent
(20%) of the Project Fee ($35,000) shall be due within three (3) business days following
Client's acceptance of Milestone 1.
(c) Milestone 2 – Backend MVP API. Upon Client's written acceptance of Milestone 2
Deliverables in accordance with Section 4.3, fifteen percent (15%) of the Project Fee
($26,250) shall be due within three (3) business days following Client's acceptance of
Milestone 2.
(d) Milestone 3 – Frontend Internal Web App. Upon Client’s written acceptance of
Milestone 3 Deliverables, in accordance with Section 4.3, fifteen percent (15%) of the
Project Fee ($26,250) shall be due within three (3) business days following Client's
acceptance of Milestone 3.
(e) Milestone 4 – Landowner Portal v1 & Hardening. Upon Client's written acceptance of
Milestone 4 Deliverables, in accordance with Section 4.3, fifteen percent (15%) of the
Project Fee ($26,250) shall be due within three (3) business days following Client's
acceptance of Milestone 4.
(f) Milestone 5 – UAT Completion & Production-Ready Package. Upon Client's written
acceptance of Milestone 5 Deliverables in accordance with Section 4.3, fifteen percent
(15%) of the Project Fee ($26,250) shall be due within three (3) business days following
Client's acceptance of Milestone 5.
5.3 Invoices. Developer shall submit an invoice upon (i) the Effective Date for the Mobilization
Fee, and (ii) Client's acceptance of each Milestone for the corresponding Milestone Fee. All
undisputed amounts shall be due and payable within three (3) business days of Client's receipt of
a valid invoice.
5.4 Expenses. Unless otherwise stated in Exhibit B, Developer's fees are inclusive of ordinary
business expenses. Any extraordinary expenses (e.g., travel, third-party tool licenses beyond the
agreed stack) must be pre-approved in writing by Client and will be invoiced at cost.
VI. Change Requests
6.1 Change Requests. Either Party may request a change to the scope, Deliverables, or schedule (a
"Change Request"). Change Requests shall be documented in writing substantially in the form
attached as Exhibit C.
(a) Material Changes Requiring Change Request. A formal Change Request is required for
any change that: (i) adds, removes, or materially modifies functionality in Exhibit A;
(ii) changes core technical architecture or technology stack (backend framework,
frontend framework, database, hosting, authentication model, or other foundational
technical decisions); (iii) would reasonably require more than 8 hours of development
time or cost more than $5,000; (iv) extends any Milestone by more than 14 days or the
overall MVP Term by more than 30 days; (v) increases the total Project Fee or any
Milestone Fee; or (vi) adds material third-party integrations, compliance requirements,
or regulatory features not in Exhibit A.
(b) Minor Changes Not Requiring Change Request. The following may be made without a
formal Change Request if they do not materially impact schedule or fees: (i)
clarifications consistent with Exhibit A; (ii) technical implementation details within
agreed stack; (iii) bug fixes; (iv) performance optimization; (v) minor UI/styling
adjustments; and (vi) security patches.
6.2 Impact Assessment. Developer shall promptly provide an assessment of the impact of any
proposed Change Request on schedule, fees, and technical risk. No Change Request shall be
binding unless signed or otherwise expressly approved in writing by both Parties.
6.3 Effect on Milestones and Fees. Approved Change Requests may adjust the Milestones,
Acceptance Criteria, and/or Project Fee. Such changes shall be reflected in an updated Exhibit B
and shall supersede any inconsistent prior schedule or fee terms.
